Why it matters for your home

Page one is where the calls happen.

Almost every family that places a loved one starts on Google, and almost none of them scroll past the first handful of results. If your home sits on page two, you might as well not be online. The homes that rank near the top get the tours, even when your care is just as good or better.

Ranking is not a one-time job. Google weighs dozens of small signals, and so do your competitors. The home two towns over is working to climb past you, and a listing that was accurate last year drifts out of date. Local SEO is the steady work that keeps your home at the top for the searches families in your city actually type, month after month.

Here is the plain case. Because almost every click goes to the top few results, ranking there for your city's searches means the family searching tonight finds you instead of a home two towns over. The numbers are not subtle: the top spot pulls roughly a third of all clicks, and page two pulls almost none.
