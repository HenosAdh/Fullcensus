The home

A real house, not a facility.

Everett Lighthouse is an adult family home on Riverview Blvd in Everett, Washington. It is a regular house on a quiet residential street, cared for by a small team, with only a handful of residents.

That size is the whole point. Your mom or dad is known by name, not by room number. Meals are cooked in the kitchen, not delivered on a tray. And when something changes, someone notices the same day.

Common questions

Questions families ask us.

What is an adult family home?

An adult family home is a regular house in a residential neighborhood, licensed by Washington State to care for a small number of adults. Because there are only a handful of residents, care is far more personal than in a large facility.

How many residents live at Everett Lighthouse?

We care for a small number of residents by design. Fewer residents means each person gets more attention, and changes get noticed early.

Is someone awake at night?

Yes. Someone is here around the clock, including overnight, so help is always immediate.

Can we visit before deciding?

Yes, and we encourage it. Walking through the home is the best way to know whether it is right for your family. Call or send a message and we will find a time that works.

What areas do you serve?

We are on Riverview Blvd in Everett, Washington, and welcome families from across Snohomish County, including Mukilteo, Marysville, Mill Creek, Lake Stevens, and Lynnwood.
